Why a Rotary Engine?

The rotary engine, while having its flaws, offers a driving experience like no other. This unique experience stems from the different manner a rotary engine goes about the combustion process. Both the standard piston engine and a rotary have four steps to combustion, those being:

  • Intake
  • Compression
  • Ignition
  • Exhaust

However, rather than having to turn linear motion into rotational motion like a piston engine, a rotary engine rotates as its compression process. This leads to smoother acceleration, as the rotary engine doesn't have to deal with pistons constantly accelerating in different directions, and in turn leads to less shifting mass within the engine as the car accelerates. In fact, one rotor has a power stroke twice as often as a cylinder of a piston engine!
